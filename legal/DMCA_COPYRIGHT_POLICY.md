### 2. How to Submit a Takedown Notice

If you believe that content on Authorr AI infringes your copyright, submit a written notice containing ALL of the following elements (required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)):

**Required Elements:**

1. **Identification of the copyrighted work:** A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed, or if multiple works are covered by a single notification, a representative list.

2. **Identification of the infringing material:** A description of the material you claim is infringing, with sufficient detail to allow us to locate it (e.g., a specific URL, account identifier, or description of where it appears).

3. **Your contact information:**
   - Full legal name
   - Physical address
   - Telephone number
   - Email address

4. **Good-faith belief statement:** A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

5. **Accuracy statement:** A statement that the information in your notice is accurate, and, **under penalty of perjury**, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

6. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature.

**Warning:** Filing a false DMCA notice is a federal offense that can result in civil and criminal penalties under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f). Only submit notices for content you genuinely believe infringes a copyright you own or control.

### 3. What Happens After We Receive Your Notice

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within a reasonable time.
2. Promptly review the notice for completeness.
3. If valid, remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing content.
4. Notify the user who posted the content (if applicable and identifiable) that the content has been removed.
5. Inform them of their right to file a counter-notice.

Incomplete notices will be rejected. We will notify you of any deficiencies so you can resubmit.

---

### 4. Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe your content was removed due to mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice under 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3). Your counter-notice must contain:

1. **Identification of the removed content:** A description of the material that was removed and the location where it appeared before removal.

2. **Statement of good faith:** A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification.

3. **Consent to jurisdiction:** A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, for users outside the United States, any judicial district in which we may be found), and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original takedown notice.

4. **Your contact information:**
   - Full legal name
   - Physical address
   - Telephone number
   - Email address

5. **Your signature:** Physical or electronic signature.

Send counter-notices to the same DMCA agent contact above.

**What happens next:** If we receive a valid counter-notice, we will:
1. Forward it to the original complainant.
2. Inform the complainant that we will restore the content within 10–14 business days.
3. Restore the content unless the complainant files a court action seeking to restrain the restoration.

## Part II — Copyright Guidance for Authorr AI Users

### 6. AI-Generated Content and Copyright

Using AI-generated content raises unique copyright considerations. Here is what you need to know:

**6.1 Copyright Status of Pure AI Output**
The U.S. Copyright Office has issued guidance stating that purely AI-generated works — where no human author made sufficiently creative selections and arrangements — are not eligible for copyright protection. This means:
- AI text generated entirely by the AI without substantial human creative input may be in the public domain from the moment of creation.
- AI audio generated entirely by voice synthesis software may not be copyrightable.

**This is not settled law and varies by jurisdiction.** Copyright law regarding AI is evolving rapidly, and other countries may have different rules.

**6.2 What You Can Do to Strengthen Your Copyright Claim**
To maximize any copyright interest you may have in AI-assisted works:
- Provide substantial, creative original direction in your prompts
- Significantly edit, curate, and arrange the AI output
- Add your own original creative elements (characters, plot, voice, style)
- Document your creative process and decisions

The more original human creativity you contribute, the stronger your potential copyright claim.

**6.3 You Cannot Copyright Others' Work**
AI systems may inadvertently reproduce text or phrases from their training data. If AI Output closely resembles a third party's copyrighted work, it does not belong to you — it may be infringing content. Always review AI Output carefully before publishing.

### 8. Using Copyrighted Material as Input

**8.1 Text Input**
Do not paste substantial portions of third-party copyrighted text into Authorr AI's writing tools without authorization. Brief quotations for transformative purposes (e.g., parody, commentary) may be permissible under fair use doctrine in the United States, but fair use is a legal defense, not a bright-line rule, and varies by jurisdiction.

**8.2 What You May Safely Input**
- Your own original writing
- Public domain text (published before 1928 in the US; check jurisdiction-specific public domain rules)
- Text licensed under Creative Commons or similar open licenses (follow the license terms)
- Brief quotations used for transformative purposes (consult an attorney if uncertain)

**8.3 Voice Recordings**
Do not upload voice recordings of third parties without their explicit consent. See the Voice Cloning Consent document for full details.

### 9. Music, Sound Effects, and Cover Art

**9.1 AI-Generated Music and SFX**
Authorr AI may generate music and sound effects via AI (Author and Studio plans). The copyright status of AI-generated music is similarly unsettled. Certain platforms (e.g., Spotify, music distributors) may have specific policies about AI-generated music. Review each platform's current rules before distributing AI-generated music.

**9.2 AI-Generated Cover Art**
Cover images generated by Authorr AI are based on your prompts. Do not use other artists' distinctive styles, names, or specific copyrighted artwork descriptions in a way intended to reproduce their protected expression. While style itself is generally not copyrightable, specific artistic elements may be.

**9.3 Using Third-Party Music or Images**
Authorr AI does not include any pre-licensed third-party music libraries. If you incorporate third-party music or images in your projects outside of our tools, you are solely responsible for obtaining appropriate licenses.
